Behaviour & Engagement Policy

▼
School Values: Ready, Respect, Safe

Students are expected to:

  • Arrive on time with correct equipment
  • Treat everyone with respect
  • Behave calmly to keep everyone safe

Good behaviour is recognised through: Praise postcards, phone calls home, celebration events

Sanctions may include: Restorative meetings, loss of privileges, detentions

Attendance Policy

▼
Target: 95%+ attendance for all students

Parents must:

  • Ensure pupils arrive on time daily
  • Report absences on the first day
  • Avoid term-time holidays (only authorised in exceptional circumstances)

Note: Penalty notices/fines may be issued for unauthorised absences

Anti-Bullying Policy

▼
Ethos: Respect, safety, and support – bullying is never tolerated

How to report:

  • In person to any member of staff
  • Anonymous drop-box
  • Email the school

Covers: Physical, verbal, online, racist, homophobic, sexist, transphobic, disability-based, and appearance-based bullying

Parents should: Talk to children, listen carefully, encourage reporting, and contact school with concerns

Keeping your children safe and healthy at school

🏥

Healthcare & First Aid

▼
Long-term medical conditions require an Individual Healthcare Plan (IHP)

If your child is injured/unwell:

  • Taken to the Ganolfan (wellbeing centre) for assessment
  • Parents contacted if child needs to go home or hospital
  • Pupils cannot phone home themselves - staff will contact you

Parents must: Provide up-to-date medical information and medication

🦠

Illness & Infection Control

▼
Common exclusion rules to prevent spread of illness

Stay home for:

  • Chickenpox: 5 days after rash appears, until spots scab over
  • Sickness/diarrhoea: At least 48 hours clear
  • Measles: 4 days after rash
  • Impetigo: Until sores healed or 48hrs after antibiotics

Contact school if child is unwell and follow NHS 111 Wales advice

Admissions Arrangements

▼
Applications required for each school stage - transfer is NOT automatic

Typical deadlines:

  • Secondary: Applications open Sept, close early Nov, offers March
  • Reception/Junior: Open Sept, close Nov, offers April
  • Nursery: Open Sept, close Feb, offers May

Oversubscription criteria: Looked After Children, SEN statements, nearest school, siblings, feeder schools, distance

Charging & Costs

▼
What's free and what may cost extra

FREE:

  • Normal school education and activities in school hours
  • School-arranged transport
  • Curriculum-based trips and exams (if pupil prepared at school)

MAY CHARGE FOR:

  • Optional activities outside school hours
  • Materials for practical subjects (if pupil keeps the product)
  • Private instrument lessons
  • Exam retakes (if not prepared at school)

Financial help available for families in certain circumstances

Complaints Procedure

▼
Clear process for raising concerns or complaints

Stage A (Informal): Speak to teacher or Headteacher's PA (Alys Crompton) - raise within 10 school days

Stage B (Formal): Investigation by senior staff/governors

Note: Pupils under 16 may complain themselves, with or without parents
